# A+ Content Planning

Choose actual Basic, Premium, or Brand Story templates in Content Manager
first. Then map factual content objectives onto their image and text boxes.

Useful objectives include:

- brand/product introduction;
- primary customer problem and use context;
- feature and material evidence;
- how-to-use sequence;
- size, fit, compatibility, or specification explanation;
- lifestyle application;
- same-brand product comparison;
- variant or family overview.

These are creative objectives, not official module names and not a mandatory
sequence. Use only the objectives supported by product evidence and the chosen
templates. Basic A+ supports at most five modules and Premium A+ at most seven.

For each image box, record:

- selected Amazon template and box label;
- exact dimensions shown by Content Manager;
- customer question answered;
- approved source facts and claims;
- source product references;
- prompt and product invariants;
- intended desktop/mobile focal area;
- alt text;
- validation status.

Keep important content away from uncertain crop edges, but confirm safe
placement in the actual desktop and mobile previews rather than relying on a
universal percentage.
